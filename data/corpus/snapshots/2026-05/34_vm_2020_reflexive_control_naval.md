# Reflexive Control of the Adversary in Naval Combat: A Psychological View

**Author:** Sitnova, Irina Valeryevna (Candidate of Sociological Sciences)
**Journal:** Военная мысль (Military Thought), 2020
**Source:** [CyberLeninka](https://cyberleninka.ru/article/n/refleksivnoe-upravlenie-protivnikom-v-usloviyah-morskogo-boya-psihologicheskiy-vzglyad-na-problemu)

## Summary

The article examines theoretical foundations for manipulating an adversary's decision-making through information-psychological operations, specifically in fourth-generation warfare contexts. While framed around naval combat, the doctrinal content applies broadly to Russian information warfare theory.

## Definition of Reflexive Control

Reflexive control (рефлексивное управление) is defined as conveying specially prepared information to adversaries "to incline them to voluntarily accept predetermined decisions favorable to the initiating side." This is the authoritative Russian formulation, rooted in Soviet-era research and operationalized in contemporary doctrine.

## Historical Development (Four Stages)

1. **1960s–1970s — Research phase:** Game theory, collective reflexology, Gestalt psychology applied to military decision analysis
2. **1980s–early 1990s — Practice-oriented phase:** Integration into operational planning; development of dedicated deception units
3. **Mid-1990s — Psychological-pedagogical phase:** Codification in military education; doctrine formalization
4. **Late 1990s onward — Psychosocial phase:** Expansion to societal-level influence; strategic communication and information environment management

## Implementation Methods

The article identifies the following as primary tools of reflexive control:
- **Military deception (военная хитрость / маскировка)** — concealment of intent, capability, and disposition
- **Demonstration of force** — signaling resolve and capability to shape adversary risk calculus
- **Disinformation (дезинформация)** — deliberate false information designed to induce adversary miscalculation
- **Panic and rumor operations** — degrading adversary cohesion and decision-making under stress
- **Manipulation of threat perceptions** — shaping adversary beliefs about costs and benefits of action

## Psychological Effects in Combat

The article identifies attention deficits, temporary memory impairment, and degraded logical reasoning as combat-induced psychological effects that reflexive control techniques are designed to exploit — particularly in time-compressed naval command environments. The same principles apply in land and information domains.

## Theoretical Basis

The technique draws on:
- Game theory (adversary modeled as rational actor whose decision inputs can be shaped)
- Field theory — decision-making as a product of environmental forces that can be manipulated
- Gestalt psychology — adversary perception as holistic and therefore susceptible to figure-ground manipulation

## Doctrinal Significance for ZARNITSA

Reflexive control is not a fringe concept — it is a core Russian operational-strategic tool. GRU, ЦВСИ, and НГШ personas should reference this framework when analyzing adversary decision-making and when recommending information operations. The key insight: Russian doctrine does not separate kinetic and informational effects — they are integrated instruments for shaping adversary decisions before the first shot is fired.
