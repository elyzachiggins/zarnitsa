# Multi-Domain Battle as a Result of the Evolution of US Joint Operations (20th–21st Centuries)

**Author:** Colonel S.I. Vodzyansky
**Journal:** Военная мысль (Military Thought), 2023
**Source:** [CyberLeninka](https://cyberleninka.ru/article/n/mnogosfernoe-srazhenie-kak-rezultat-evolyutsii-sovmestnyh-deystviy-razlichnyh-vidov-vooruzhennyh-sil-ssha-v-hh-hhi-vekah)

## Summary

A Russian General Staff-affiliated analysis of the evolution of US joint operational concepts from the Cold War through the Multi-Domain Battle (MDB) framework, examining the 2025–2040 strategic framework for integrated US military operations.

## Two Phases of 21st-Century US Strategy (Russian Framing)

- **2001–2010:** Preventive, unilateral offensive operations — "shock and awe," regime change, direct military action
- **2010–present:** "Managed chaos" and hybrid warfare — globally integrated campaigns designed to achieve effects without direct military confrontation; emphasis on proxy forces, information operations, economic coercion

## Multi-Domain Battle Concept (Russian Assessment)

The author identifies four primary objectives of the MDB concept:
1. Achieving superiority before armed conflict
2. Transforming denied-access/area-denial (A2/AD) zones into contested areas
3. Disrupting enemy designs across all domains simultaneously
4. Consolidating success after degradation of adversary integrated defense

**Five operational domains identified:** Physical (land, sea, air, space), electromagnetic spectrum, cyberspace, information environment, and sociocultural dimensions.

## Force Structure Innovation (Russian Concern)

The assessment highlights US development of "semi-autonomous formations" capable of distributed operations across multiple domains simultaneously — replacing brigade-centric organization with divisional structures capable of independent multi-domain action. This is assessed as specifically designed to overwhelm Russian layered defense.

## Russian Countermeasure Logic

The author recommends identifying "the most vulnerable aspects of the opponent and realizing all available capabilities for inflicting maximum damage" — a formulation consistent with Russian reflexive control doctrine and the principle of striking asymmetrically at adversary decision nodes rather than matching capability for capability.
