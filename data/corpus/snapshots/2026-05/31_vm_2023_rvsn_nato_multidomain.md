# Role of Strategic Missile Forces in Countering NATO's Strategic Multi-Domain Operation

**Authors:** General-Lieutenant I.R. Fazletdinov (Candidate of Military Sciences); Colonel (Ret.) V.I. Lumpov (Doctor of Military Sciences)
**Journal:** Военная мысль (Military Thought), №3, 2023
**Source:** [CyberLeninka](https://cyberleninka.ru/article/n/rol-raketnyh-voysk-strategicheskogo-naznacheniya-v-protivodeystvii-strategicheskoy-mnogosfernoy-operatsii-nato)

## Summary

The article characterizes NATO's strategic multi-domain operation — its objectives, tasks, operational content, and resulting threats to RF national security — and proposes countermeasures employing Russia's Strategic Missile Forces (РВСН).

## Core Argument

Nuclear weapons remain the primary and most economical deterrent mechanism in a multipolar world where traditional strategic stability mechanisms are eroding. The authors argue that Russia can maintain strategic deterrence by demonstrating the capability to prevent critical losses to its nuclear arsenal during any conflict phase.

## NATO's Strategic Multi-Domain Concept (Russian Assessment)

NATO's multi-domain operational concept integrates military and civilian capabilities across geographical, geophysical, and virtual domains — including space, information, and cyber domains. The Russian authors identify this as a framework designed to achieve decision superiority before armed conflict, transform denied-access zones into contested areas, disrupt Russian strategic designs, and consolidate success across all domains simultaneously.

## Three-Phase Conflict Scenario (Russian Model)

The authors outline a proposed adversary conflict structure:
1. **Preparatory period** — hybrid operations, information warfare, proxy activation, degradation of Russian C2
2. **Primary period** — air-space operations targeting nuclear infrastructure, C2 nodes, and logistics
3. **Concluding period** — ground operations following degradation of Russian strategic forces

## Russian Countermeasure Framework

The article proposes a "Strategic Forces Deterrence Operation" employing all RF military components across three functional phases. The РВСН role is to demonstrate survivable second-strike capability sufficient to impose unacceptable costs on any adversary even after absorbing a first strike. Key elements:
- Dispersal and hardening of mobile ICBM forces (Ярс, Тополь-М systems)
- Coordinated deterrence signaling across strategic triad
- Integration with Aerospace Forces (ВКС) for early warning
- Non-nuclear precision strike as a sub-nuclear deterrence layer

## Doctrinal Significance

This article reflects current General Staff thinking on how nuclear deterrence integrates with conventional multi-domain competition. The framing — that NATO's multi-domain concept is explicitly designed to neutralize Russian nuclear forces before escalation — directly informs Russian escalation thresholds and the logic of nuclear signaling in conventional conflict.
