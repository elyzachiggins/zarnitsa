# Analysis of Leading States' Concepts for Employing Advanced Non-Nuclear Means of Destruction

**Authors:** Colonel Yaroslav V. Bespalov; Colonel Mikhail L. Tikhonov (Candidate of Military Sciences)
**Journal:** Военная мысль (Military Thought), 2022
**Source:** [CyberLeninka](https://cyberleninka.ru/article/n/analiz-kontseptsiy-veduschih-gosudarstv-po-primeneniyu-perspektivnyh-neyadernyh-sredstv-porazheniya)

## Summary

The authors examine how US and NATO doctrinal frameworks have shifted from traditional nuclear deterrence toward developing non-nuclear strategic strike capabilities — and assess the implications for Russian national security.

## Core Finding

The US intends to develop effective non-nuclear strategic military deterrence capability using rapid-strike systems and directed-energy weapons as transitional tools — ultimately aiming to replace nuclear deterrence with precision conventional overmatch. The authors characterize this as presenting "significant negative consequences" for Russian national security requiring active countermeasures.

## US/NATO Strategic Concepts Examined

- **Strategic Deterrence Operations** — integrating nuclear and conventional instruments to deter and, if necessary, defeat adversaries
- **Global Strike concept (Prompt Global Strike / PGS)** — rapid conventional strike against any target globally within one hour
- **Large-Scale Joint Military Operations** — combined arms theater warfare
- **Future Joint Operations** — multi-domain integrated campaign design

## Technology Assessment

The authors identify the following as the primary tools of non-nuclear strategic strike:
- **Hypersonic glide vehicles** (HGV) — capable of defeating current Russian ballistic missile defense
- **Submarine-launched cruise missiles** (SLCM) — stealthy delivery against hardened targets
- **Directed-energy weapons** — for disabling satellite and C2 infrastructure
- **Advanced conventional ballistic missiles** — sub-nuclear precision engagement of hardened facilities

## Russian Doctrinal Response

The article implies that Russia's response must be asymmetric — that matching the US in non-nuclear precision strike is economically unsustainable. The preferred Russian approach is to:
1. Maintain credible nuclear escalation as a deterrent to non-nuclear strategic attack
2. Develop its own hypersonic delivery systems (Кинжал, Циркон, Авангард) as a counter
3. Invest in EW and kinetic ASAT to defeat adversary ISR and C2 enabling precision strike

## Doctrinal Significance

This article captures the Russian assessment of the "strategic stability erosion" narrative that underpins current nuclear doctrine updates. The framing — that non-nuclear precision strike threatens to make nuclear weapons redundant — directly motivates Russia's lower nuclear threshold doctrine and its emphasis on demonstrative nuclear use as an escalation control tool.
